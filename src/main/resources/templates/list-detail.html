Traction substation

4 Comments 4 Comments 2 comments

Traction substation - an electrical installation designed to lower the electrical voltage and then convert (rectify) the current (for DC substations) in order to transfer it to the contact network to provide electrical energy to electric locomotives, electric trains, trams and trolleybuses. Traction substations are of direct and alternating current.

Traction substations for trams and trolley buses are used to convert three-phase alternating current (usually 6 or 10 kV) into direct current. DC voltage for urban electric transport in most cities of the world is accepted: on the pantograph of tram and trolleybus 550 V, on the buses of traction substations 600 V. Traction substations are powered by 6 or 10 kV overhead or cable lines from the power system.
